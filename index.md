        <h1 class="title">Sophie Huiberts</h1>
        <l>Researcher @ CNRS</l>
        <p>
                    I am a <a href="https://www.cnrs.fr/en">CNRS researcher</a> since fall 2023 and I am
                    hosted at <a href="https://limos.fr/">LIMOS, Clermont Auvergne University</a>
                    in Clermont-Ferrand.
                    Previously, I was
                    a <a href="https://www.simonsfoundation.org/simons-society-of-fellows">Simons Junior Fellow</a>
                    at <a href="https://theory.cs.columbia.edu/">Columbia University</a>
                    in NYC hosted by <a href="https://timroughgarden.org/">Tim Roughgarden</a>.
                    I did my PhD research
                    at <a href="https://www.cwi.nl/en/groups/networks-and-optimization/">Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica</a>
                    in Amsterdam
                    under <a href="https://homepages.cwi.nl/~dadush/">Daniel Dadush</a>
                    and received my doctorate in 2022
                    from <a href="https://www.uu.nl/en/organisation/mathematical-institute">Utrecht University</a>.</p>

                    <p>In my work, I prove theorems about algorithms.
                    Specifically, I study practical algorithms for (mixed integer) linear programming as you might find them
                    in popular software such as Gurobi, FICO Xpress, SCIP or HiGHS.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                    These mathematical optimization tools are used extensively
                    in industry and academia and are very fast in practice.
                    This observation is in contrast with the theory of worst-case analysis,
                    which predicts that the algorithms in these solvers would be slow.
                    My research aims to narrow this gap between practical
                    observation and theoretical prediction.
                    Most of my work is about the simplex method.
                    </p>

                    <p>
                    For a longer introduction to my research, check out
                    <a href="/files/research-statement-huiberts-web.pdf">my research statement</a>,
                    More information on my professional activities can be found in
                    <a href="/files/cv-huiberts.pdf">my CV</a>.
                    </p>
